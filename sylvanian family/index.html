Into the idyllic world of Sylvanian Families.

By Michelle Sheen

Source: Sylvanian Families

Created in 1985 by the Japanese company Epoch, the Sylvanian Families toy began selling in the UK two years later in 1987.

Almost instantly, the anthropomorphic figurines gained immense popularity — even being named "Toy of the Year" between 1987 and 1989 in the UK.

Though considered a wholesome relic of the past, the Sylvanian Families collections and figurines remain popular among both children and adults for its whimsical setting and detailed accessories. Today, these beloved dolls are sold in more than 70 countries, and the brand has witnessed over 200 million sales since its initial release.

“They give me comfort.” - Koiki Tagauchi

According to The Independent, collectors and lovers of the toy can find a wide variety of animals — from mice and rabbits to otters, hedgehogs and beavers.

“In the world of Sylvania, nothing bad ever happens,” says one worker at a Sylvanian Families shop in London, describing the allure of the homely toys.

One article by the Straits Times delves into the resurgence of the little dolls’ popularity in recent years, especially among Japanese women.

“I enjoy traveling with them and making clothes for them,” said Koiki Tagauchi, a university student in Tokyo. “They give me comfort.”
